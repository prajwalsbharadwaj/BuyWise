# Structural Inspection (INSP_STRUCTURAL)

**Category:** Professional Expert Service  
**Partner Type:** Civil Engineer / Surveyor  
**Estimated SLA:** 2–4 Working Days  

## 1. Purpose

**Why is Structural Inspection required?**  
To assess the physical health, construction quality, and safety of a property before purchase. It uncovers hidden flaws that aren't visible to an untrained eye.

**When should it be executed?**  
During the Evaluation phase, ideally before finalizing the price or signing the Sale Agreement.

**How is it different from Property Valuation?**  
Valuation determines the financial worth of the property. Structural Inspection determines the physical integrity and repair costs.

**What legal protection does it provide?**  
It provides grounds to renegotiate the price or back out of a deal if severe structural defects or unauthorized deviations are found.

**When can disputes arise?**  
If the surveyor causes damage during inspection, or if the seller refuses entry.

---

## 2. Eligibility

**Who can request it?**
- Buyers evaluating an independent house, villa, or older apartment.
- Buyers taking handover of a new apartment from a builder (Snagging).

**Unsupported scenarios:**
- Empty plots of land (requires Land Survey, not Structural Inspection).
- Properties where the current occupant/owner refuses physical access.

## 7. Human Partner Responsibilities

The assigned Civil Engineer must:
- Coordinate with the site contact and visit the property on time.
- Conduct a thorough visual and non-destructive inspection.
- Check for:
  - Seepage, dampness, and plumbing leaks.
  - Structural cracks in columns, beams, or load-bearing walls.
  - Electrical safety (earthing, wiring age).
  - Deviation from approved building plans (setback violations).
- Provide estimated remediation costs for any defects found.
- Upload a detailed Inspection Report with photographs.

---
